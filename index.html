Though the U.S. Constitution forbid Transatlantic slave trade after 1808, this only increased the domestic American industry of buying, selling and "breeding" slaves. Some say that the ban on the slave trade was in fact to diminish foreign competition from African slaves. These circumstances meant that after 1808 , the slave trade surged in Washington.
