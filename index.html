--- layout: default title: World Heritage --- Mapping Slave Sites in Washington DC

Though the U.S. Constitution forbid Transatlantic slave trade after 1808, this only increased the domestic American slave industry. Slave traders and entire companies made money off of buying and selling enslaved African Americans, and sometimes even capturing free African Americans. Domestic American slave traders benefited from the 1808 ban, because it eliminated the competition from foreign slave traders. As a result, the slave trade boomed in Washington DC.
